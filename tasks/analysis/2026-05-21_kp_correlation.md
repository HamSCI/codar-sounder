## Findings

### 1. σ_φ thresholds are too low (likely 2× too low)

At Kp = 1.00 — the quietest 3-hour bucket in the dataset — σ_φ
averages **1.27 rad** and 77% of peaks still classify as "strong".
At Kp = 3.00 (unsettled), σ_φ averages 1.43 rad and 86% are
"strong".  The 12-hour scintillation-enabled window doesn't include
a real storm, but already shows that the v0.5.2 thresholds (weak <
0.5, moderate < 1.0, strong ≥ 1.0) saturate at "strong" essentially
all the time.

The HF intrinsic phase-incoherence floor at SEAB / 13.45 MHz /
1416 km appears to sit around 1.2-1.5 rad, *not* below 1.0 rad.
v0.5.2's recalibration was correct in direction but insufficient
in magnitude.  Suggested v0.6.2 bump: weak < 1.5, moderate < 2.0.
Definitive calibration awaits a storm with v0.5+ logging — Kp ≥ 5
is needed to confirm strong-event behaviour at the new thresholds.

### 2. F2_extreme rate is suspiciously high (~35%) and decoupled from Kp

Across 60 3-hour buckets covering quiet days, a G2 storm
(2026-05-15 21:00 UTC, Kp = 6.33), and the recovery:

| Kp level | F2_extreme% | mean h' (km) | mean SNR (dB) |
|---|---|---|---|
| quiet (Kp < 3) | 37.6 | 455 | — |
| unsettled (3–4) | 36.8 | 452 | — |
| active (4–5) | 35.6 | 443 | — |
| G1 (5–6) | 30.5 | 417 | — |
| G2 (≥ 6) | 39.5 | 476 | 33.5 |

The rate is essentially constant — not what we'd expect if it were
a real ionospheric-disturbance signature.  Hypothesis: ``invert()``
is misclassifying **3-hop returns** as 1-hop with implausibly high
h'.  At SEAB's 1416 km ground distance:

  - 3-hop at h_true=250 km → group_range ≈ 2064 km
  - Secant-law 1-hop inversion of group_range=2064 km, ground=1416 km
    → h_apparent ≈ 751 km → "F2_extreme"

If 35% of detections at SEAB are actually 3-hop returns being
mislabeled, then virtual_height_km / mode_layer / EVF are
unreliable for that population.  Worth investigating before any
downstream consumer trusts the F2_extreme classification.

### 3. The G2 storm DID move metrics, but slightly

During the May 15 21:00 UTC bucket (G2):

  - F2_extreme: 39.5% (vs 37.6% quiet baseline; +1.9 pp)
  - mean h': 476 km (vs 455 km; +21 km)
  - mean SNR: 33.5 dB (vs ~37 dB quiet baseline; -3.5 dB)
  - CPIs: 663 (vs typical 720; -8% — some sweeps probably failed
    peak detection due to absorption)

So the receiver IS sensitive to the storm, but the F2_extreme
"signal" is small (+1.9 pp shift) and goes the wrong direction
from the multi-hop misclassification hypothesis.  Mean SNR drop
and CPI-count drop are consistent with HF absorption during the
storm — a more reliable disturbance proxy than F2_extreme rate.

## Recommendations

1. **v0.6.2 (this session)**: bump σ_φ severity thresholds to
   weak < 1.5, moderate < 2.0, strong ≥ 2.0.  Document that
   these are HF-intrinsic-floor-calibrated and may need a final
   pass once a v0.5+-era storm is observed.

2. **F2_extreme investigation (separate diagnostic)**: write a
   script that loads F2_extreme records, computes the apparent
   group_range, and checks whether 2-hop / 3-hop / 4-hop
   re-interpretations land in normal F2 height range.  If yes,
   ``invert()`` should consider multi-hop modes (significant
   work — defer the actual fix to a later release).

3. **Re-run this analysis in 1-2 weeks**: more v0.5+ data will
   accumulate and ideally span a wider Kp range.  Re-running
   ``scripts/kp_correlation_analysis.py`` will refresh the
   findings without code changes.
